starbucks versioning

tl;dr

Given a version number venti.grande.tall, increment the:

  1. venti version when you make incompatible code changes,
  2. grande version when you add functionality in a backwards compatible manner, and
  3. tall version when you make backwards compatible bug fixes.

Additional labels for pre-release and build metadata are available as extensions to the venti.grande.tall format.

cool, but why?

every dev knows the situation of trying to fix a bug for a whole freaking day. when you then suddenly are able to solve it (or declare it as a feature), you are excited to merge it into production.
but just as a grande update or tall? isn't that depressing? i mean, you worked your ass off just for a "grande" update?!
that's why i invented starbucks versioning (well, it's basically just semantic versioning, but way better). now you didn't create a grande update or tall, you created a grande or tall update! (cool, eh?) this sounds way cooler and also boosts your motivation. when telling your one friend that you merged a minor update into production, he'd probably say "whatever", but if you tell him that you merged a grande update into production, he'd probably say something like "amazing! but why do you use italian words?"
also, as a coffee lover (as every dev is, right?), the fancy-sounding words venti, grande and tall are by far easier to remember than those strange, english-sounding words major, minor and patch
